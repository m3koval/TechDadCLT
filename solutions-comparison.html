Digital Protection Solutions —
What's available and what it actually does

A practical comparison of every layer of protection we can put in place. Free built-in tools, whole-home network controls, and third-party monitoring apps — and where each one falls short on its own.

Tier 1

Free Built-In Controls

Every major platform ships with parental controls. They're free, reasonably effective, and already on your devices. The gap: they have almost no visibility — you can set rules but you won't know what was attempted, bypassed, or slipping through.

Apple Screen Time Google Family Link Amazon Kids Console Controls
Key limitation: No alerts, no reports, no visibility. You set it and hope it's working.
Tier 2

Whole-Home Network Controls

Router and network-level filtering covers every device on your WiFi — phones, tablets, game consoles, smart TVs, even friends' devices when they visit. Powerful inside the home. The gap: the moment a device switches to cellular data, it bypasses everything.

Eero Plus Circle Gryphon CleanBrowsing
Key limitation: Home WiFi only. Cellular data, school networks, and friends' WiFi are completely unprotected.
Tier 3

Third-Party Monitoring Apps

Device-installed apps that follow your child everywhere — home WiFi, school network, cellular data, anywhere. Provide real visibility: activity reports, real-time alerts, and in some cases AI-powered monitoring of texts, emails, and social media for concerning content.

Bark Qustodio Aura Life360
Key limitation: Monthly subscription required. Must be installed on each device individually.
Tier 1 — Free Built-In Controls
Apple · Free
Apple Screen Time
Free
Built into iOS / iPadOS / macOS
Apple's native parental control system. Tightly integrated with the Apple ecosystem — the best free option for iPhone and iPad families.
  • App limits & downtime scheduling
  • Content & web filtering
  • Communication limits (calls/texts)
  • App purchase restrictions
  • No activity reports or alerts
  • Apple devices only
  • Can be bypassed with second Apple ID
Google · Free
Google Family Link
Free
Android / Chromebook
Google's family management platform for Android devices and Chromebooks. Managed from a parent app. Solid for younger children; supervision ends automatically at 13.
  • App approval & blocking
  • Screen time limits & schedules
  • Location tracking
  • SafeSearch & YouTube restrictions
  • No activity reports or alerts
  • Android / Chromebook only
  • Supervision ends automatically at age 13
Amazon · Free w/ Prime
Amazon Kids+
$3–5/mo after trial
Free on Fire tablets
Excellent for young children on Amazon Fire tablets. Age-gated content library, time limits, and a kid-friendly interface. Very limited outside the Amazon ecosystem.
  • Curated content library by age
  • Daily time limits & bedtime
  • Basic usage reports
  • Fire tablets & Echo only
  • Not designed for teens
Console · Free
Nintendo / PlayStation / Xbox Controls
Free
Native to each console
Each major gaming platform has built-in parental controls, manageable from a parent app. Often overlooked — game consoles have browsers, messaging, and online strangers.
  • Content rating restrictions
  • Online multiplayer / chat controls
  • Spending limits
  • Playtime limits (Switch)
  • No activity alerts
  • Each console configured separately
Tier 2 — Whole-Home Network Controls
Router Add-On
Eero Plus
$9.99/mo
Requires Eero router
Built directly into Eero mesh routers. If the family already has Eero hardware, this is the lowest-friction network-level solution — no extra device needed.
  • Covers every device on WiFi
  • Profiles per device or person
  • Content category blocking
  • Scheduled internet pauses
  • Home WiFi only — no cellular coverage
  • Limited activity reporting
Hardware + Subscription
Circle
$9.99/mo + hardware
Works with most routers
Dedicated device that plugs into your network and filters all WiFi traffic. More detailed reporting than Eero. Works with most routers so no hardware upgrade needed.
  • Works with any router
  • Per-device time limits & schedules
  • Detailed website category reports
  • Limited off-network (Circle app, extra cost)
  • Home WiFi primary — cellular bypasses it
  • Extra hardware to maintain
Router · Built-In
Gryphon
$150–200 hardware
Subscription included
A mesh router with parental controls baked in — no separate device needed. Replaces the existing router entirely. Strong content filtering and decent reporting.
  • All-in-one router + controls
  • Advanced content filtering
  • Per-profile time limits
  • Higher upfront cost
  • Home WiFi only
DNS · Free Tier
CleanBrowsing / OpenDNS
Free basic tier
Paid from $20/yr
DNS-level filtering — the most lightweight network option. Blocks categories of sites before they even load. Free tier is solid for basic adult content blocking across all home devices.
  • Covers all devices on network
  • Free tier available
  • No extra hardware needed
  • Limited reporting (paid only)
  • Requires router config — more technical
  • Home WiFi only

Recommended Combinations by Situation

🧒 Young kids (under 10)

Apple Screen Time Amazon Kids+ Eero Plus or Circle

Free built-ins configured correctly + network filtering covers the home. Monitoring apps aren't necessary yet — focus is on blocking and limits, not surveillance.

📱 Tweens (10–13)

Screen Time / Family Link Circle or Eero Plus Bark

This is the highest-risk window — social media starts, online friends appear, content gets more complex. Bark's AI monitoring adds the visibility that free tools completely lack.

🎮 Teens with gaming heavy households

Console Native Controls Eero Plus or Circle Bark or Qustodio

Console controls on each device + network-level filtering to catch everything the console controls miss + a monitoring app for what happens outside the home.

🔒 Maximum protection (any age)

Built-in controls Network filtering Qustodio

All three layers working together. Built-ins handle device-level rules, network covers the home, Qustodio provides full visibility and protection everywhere the device goes.

Senior & Aging Parent Protection

Different tools apply for seniors. The goal isn't parental controls — it's blocking scam vectors, filtering malicious content, and reducing the attack surface on their devices and accounts.

Call Screening · Free/Paid
Hiya / Robokiller
Free–$4/mo
Identifies and blocks scam, spam, and robocalls before they ring. One of the highest-impact interventions for seniors — most scam attempts start with a phone call.
  • Scam call identification & blocking
  • Robocall screening
  • Works on iPhone and Android
Identity Protection · Paid
Aura / LifeLock
$12–25/mo
Monitors credit, financial accounts, and personal information for signs of fraud or identity theft. Sends alerts when suspicious activity is detected and provides recovery support.
  • Credit & financial monitoring
  • Dark web alerts
  • Identity theft recovery support
Browser Safety · Free
Built-in Browser Settings
Free
Google Safe Browsing, Microsoft Defender SmartScreen, and browser-level phishing protection are already built into Chrome, Edge, and Safari — but they need to be verified as enabled.
  • Phishing site warnings
  • Malware download blocking
  • Free — just needs to be turned on
Account Security · Free
Two-Factor Authentication + Password Manager
Free – $3/mo
Enabling 2FA on email, banking, and social accounts is the single most effective way to prevent account takeover, even if a password is stolen. A simple password manager prevents reuse.
  • Stops account takeover even with stolen password
  • Works on every account type
  • Free on most platforms
