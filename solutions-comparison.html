Digital Protection Solutions —
What's available and what it actually does

A practical comparison of every layer of protection we can put in place. Free built-in tools, whole-home network controls, and third-party monitoring apps — and where each one falls short on its own.

Last updated: April 2025 — pricing and platform behavior verified
Tier 1

Free Built-In Controls

Every major platform ships with parental controls. They're free, reasonably effective, and already on your devices. The gap: they have almost no visibility — you can set rules but you won't know what was attempted, bypassed, or slipping through.

Apple Screen Time Google Family Link Amazon Kids Console Controls
Key limitation: No alerts, no reports, no visibility. You set it and hope it's working.
Tier 2

Whole-Home Network Controls

Router and network-level filtering covers every device on your WiFi — phones, tablets, game consoles, smart TVs, even friends' devices when they visit. Powerful inside the home. The gap: the moment a device switches to cellular data, it bypasses everything.

Eero Plus Circle Gryphon CleanBrowsing
Key limitation: Home WiFi only. Cellular data, school networks, and friends' WiFi are completely unprotected.
Tier 3

Third-Party Monitoring Apps

Device-installed apps that follow your child everywhere — home WiFi, school network, cellular data, anywhere. Provide real visibility: activity reports, real-time alerts, and in some cases AI-powered monitoring of texts, emails, and social media for concerning content.

Bark Qustodio Canopy Aura Life360
Key limitation: Monthly subscription required. Must be installed on each device individually. iOS/iPad caveat: Apple restricts background monitoring — no third-party app can continuously scan device content on iOS. Apps handle this differently; see the iOS Reality Check below.

Tier 3 — Third-Party Monitoring Apps
iOS / iPad Reality Check — this affects every app below

Apple's privacy policies prevent any third-party app from continuously monitoring device content in the background on iPhone or iPad. This is an Apple platform restriction — not a flaw in any specific app. The practical effect: on iOS, deep message and social media scanning requires a sync event (home WiFi, desktop app, or a sync cable). Web filtering and app blocking, however, can run continuously via a VPN/device profile and travel with the device everywhere. The apps below handle this differently — Bark relies entirely on sync; Qustodio and Canopy use a device profile for always-on content filtering.

Best for Android / Older Teens
Bark
$14/mo or $99/yr
Unlimited devices
AI-powered monitoring that reads texts, emails, and 30+ social media platforms for signs of bullying, depression, predators, and explicit content. Alerts only when something concerning is found. Best on Android — on iOS/iPad, device-level monitoring requires a home sync, but cloud accounts (Gmail, iCloud email, Google Drive, WhatsApp, GroupMe) are monitored anywhere since they sync through the cloud, not device WiFi.
  • AI monitoring of texts, email, 30+ social platforms
  • Cloud accounts (Gmail, Drive, WhatsApp) monitored anywhere — no WiFi needed
  • Android: fully live monitoring on cellular everywhere
  • Location tracking, screen time & web filtering
  • iOS/iPad device content: retroactive scan at home sync only
  • iOS: TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, Discord — zero coverage (Apple blocks all apps)
iOS families note: Bark's value on iPad is real — but narrow. Cloud account monitoring works everywhere. Device content monitoring is retroactive (catches it when they get home). Bark's own solution is the Bark Phone (Android hardware they fully control). For iPad content protection away from home, pair with Qustodio or Gryphon HomeBound.

Recommended Combinations by Situation

🧒 Young kids (under 10) — iPad household

Apple Screen Time Gryphon AX Qustodio or Canopy

Screen Time handles device-level rules. Gryphon covers the whole home including consoles and TVs. Qustodio or Canopy adds always-on content filtering that travels with the iPad on cellular — the gap Gryphon can't cover away from home.

📱 Tweens (10–13) — iPad + Android mix

Screen Time / Family Link Gryphon AX Bark (Android) + Qustodio (iPad)

This age is the highest-risk window. On Android, Bark's live AI monitoring is the right tool. On iPad, Bark's iOS sync model limits outside-home coverage — Qustodio's device profile fills that gap with continuous filtering everywhere.

🎮 Gaming-heavy household (consoles + iPads)

Console Native Controls Gryphon AX Qustodio

Console controls on each device + Gryphon catches everything the console controls miss at the network level + Qustodio covers iPads/phones both home and away. Gryphon's network layer handles the Switch and smart TVs that apps can't touch.

🔒 Maximum protection — content safety first

Screen Time (configured) Gryphon AX Qustodio + Canopy

Three full layers: Screen Time for device rules, Gryphon for the home network, Qustodio for continuous device-profile filtering everywhere + location, and Canopy adds AI visual content detection for explicit images that slip past URL blocking.

🧓 Aging parent / senior safety

Aura + Circle Hiya / Robokiller 2FA + Password Mgr

Aura handles network filtering at home (via Circle), device-level controls, identity theft monitoring, and dark web alerts — all in one subscription. Add Hiya for call screening and 2FA on key accounts and you've covered the three biggest scam vectors.

🏠 Full household — kids and an aging parent

Aura + Circle (covers everyone) Bark (kids monitoring) Hiya (senior phones)

This is the scenario where Aura's hybrid architecture earns its keep. Circle's network filtering protects every device in the house — kids and parent alike. Bark adds AI-powered content monitoring for children's devices. Hiya screens calls on the senior's phone. One subscription handles the network layer for the whole house; targeted apps fill the gaps for each audience.

Senior & Aging Parent Protection

Different tools apply for seniors. The goal isn't parental controls — it's blocking scam vectors, filtering malicious content, and reducing the attack surface on their devices and accounts.

Call Screening · Free/Paid
Hiya / Robokiller
Free–$4/mo
Identifies and blocks scam, spam, and robocalls before they ring. One of the highest-impact interventions for seniors — most scam attempts start with a phone call.
  • Scam call identification & blocking
  • Robocall screening
  • Works on iPhone and Android
Identity + Family Safety · Paid
Aura
$12–37/mo
The standout option for seniors in a household that also has kids — Aura acquired Circle in 2021, so one subscription covers network-level device filtering at home and identity theft protection. Unusual combination that no other product matches.
  • Credit & financial account monitoring
  • Dark web & data breach alerts
  • Identity theft recovery + $1M insurance
  • Circle network filtering (built in)
  • Antivirus + VPN on all devices
Identity Protection · Paid
LifeLock (by Norton)
$9–30/mo
Norton's identity protection product — well-known, widely trusted, and solid for standalone identity monitoring. A good fit if the household doesn't need Circle's network controls and wants a brand seniors may already recognize.
  • Credit bureau monitoring
  • Dark web surveillance
  • Identity theft recovery support
  • No network filtering (identity-only)
Browser Safety · Free
Built-in Browser Settings
Free
Google Safe Browsing, Microsoft Defender SmartScreen, and browser-level phishing protection are already built into Chrome, Edge, and Safari — but they need to be verified as enabled.
  • Phishing site warnings
  • Malware download blocking
  • Free — just needs to be turned on
Account Security · Free
Two-Factor Authentication + Password Manager
Free – $3/mo
Enabling 2FA on email, banking, and social accounts is the single most effective way to prevent account takeover, even if a password is stolen. A simple password manager prevents reuse.
  • Stops account takeover even with stolen password
  • Works on every account type
  • Free on most platforms
